About this blog

For the first time after 3 and a half years in university, I finally find time to enjoy life again. No more gruelling 6-to-9 weekdays of classes and ACM-ICPC training. No more brutal semesters with 7 projects hammering down at the same time - sure, my thesis is still there, but it's pretty cushy once I am done with the DL model. And I even got myself a pretty good job, too, so I don't need to worry about what to do once I go independent from my parents.

Now, I have all the time in the world to do all the things I wanted to do. All the things that I missed out after all these years! Reading the novels I bought from Singapore, playing the library of games I stacked up in my Steam account, my PS4 and Switch, or reuniting with long lost friends. The possibilities are endless!

But instead of doing any of that, let's make another blog!

It's interesting how every once in a while, the idea of writing would come back to me. I would think about ways to revitalize my old blog Cowboy Coder, and would go so far as to redesign and code modern versions of it myself - this website was actually my second attempt at doing just that. But once the technical part is finished, I would always be too busy to migrate posts from the old site to the new, let alone writing new posts, and the new design always ended up recycled for a different purpose or abandoned.

This time, after one and half a year in the unused dust bin, I decided to turn this blog theme into my personal blog.

What to expect

I think I'm mostly known for my hardcore passion for computer programming and my achievements as a student. But outside of those, I'm also a normal guy with hobbies and feelings too but we don't talk too much about that. I love techs and games - which are pretty basic I guess, and don't stray too far away from my job - but I love them nevertheless. I would love to talk about how a new smart device attracted my attention or how a game deeply touched my soul. But I know not a whole lot of people on my friendlist would be interested in such posts. Worst case scenario, they may get the wrong impression of me being an arrogant, bragging smartass if I keep talking about the toys I bought.

By posting about these on my personal blog, I will not clog up other people's news feed, while still be able to write about the things I like to my heart's content. It's my personal safe space.

Expect:

  • Shameless flexes of techs I own and my thoughts about them.
  • Reviews about songs, movies and games that touched my soul (especially Kingdom Hearts and The Legend of Zelda, I'm deep into these).
  • Personal stories of my life.
  • Software development too. I love writing about it, so I may share new things I learned on this blog.
  • Shitposts.

I had two edgy blogs before and I freaking hate those although I think everyone hates their past childish selves to a certain degree so I promise I will try my best to keep this one edge-free and non-cynical. But do expect snarky remarks because I love sarcasm.

Andddd I guess that's it. Go read my blog posts - I heard they are very interesting, more so than this introduction page :D
